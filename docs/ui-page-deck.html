Design deck · v1

Every screen in the scheduling app

Sixteen pages across two roles. Employee pages are phone-first; manager pages are desktop-first but responsive. Visual style: calm and minimal (Option A) with the home screen built as a decision feed (Option D). One rule everywhere: the system's internal words never reach the screen.

Status language — what employees see instead of engineering terms

Internal (code and tickets)On screen
tier1_broadcastAsking your team at Kingsway · 4 people
tier2_broadcastAsking staff at other locations
escalatedSent to your manager
coveredCovered by Jordan — your day off is approved
coverage_request (day_off, pending)Finding someone to cover you
manager_resolved / cancelledSorted by your manager / Request cancelled

Progress is shown as three dots — Your team → Other locations → Manager — so nobody needs to learn what a "tier" is.

Not building (on purpose)

No separate chat interface (the SMS channel already is the conversation — an in-app chat like Option E is a phase-2 experiment). No employee directory browsing, no messaging between staff, no time-clock/punch-in — out of scope for MVP, and the first two would conflict with the privacy rule.
